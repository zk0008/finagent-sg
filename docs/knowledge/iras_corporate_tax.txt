IRAS Corporate Income Tax Guide for Singapore Companies

SOURCE: Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore (IRAS), Income Tax Act

---

CORPORATE INCOME TAX (CIT) RATE

The corporate income tax rate in Singapore is a flat 17% of chargeable income.
This flat rate applies to both local and foreign companies.
There is no graduated tax rate for companies — the 17% applies to all levels of chargeable income.

---

CHARGEABLE INCOME

Chargeable income = accounting profit + add-backs - deductions - tax exemptions

Add-backs (non-deductible expenses added back to accounting profit):
- Depreciation (replaced by capital allowances)
- Private motor vehicle expenses (S-plate cars): 100% disallowed
- Entertainment expenses not wholly and exclusively incurred in production of income
- Penalties and fines
- Capital losses
- Expenses not incurred in producing income

Deductions (expenses allowed against income):
- Revenue expenses wholly and exclusively incurred in producing income
- Staff costs (salaries, CPF, bonuses)
- Rental of business premises
- Utilities
- Repairs and maintenance
- Professional fees (audit, accounting, legal — if revenue in nature)
- Bad debts written off (if revenue in nature and previously included in income)
- Capital allowances (in lieu of depreciation)

Capital allowances (Section 19/19A of Income Tax Act):
- Writing down allowance (WDA) over 3 years for qualifying plant and machinery
- 1-year write-off for low-value items (cost ≤ SGD 5,000 per item, up to SGD 30,000 total per year)
- Accelerated capital allowances available under certain schemes

---

TAX EXEMPTION SCHEMES

New Start-Up Tax Exemption (SUTE)
Applies to qualifying new companies for the first 3 consecutive years of assessment from year of incorporation.
Conditions:
- Incorporated in Singapore
- Tax resident in Singapore
- Has no more than 20 shareholders throughout the basis period; or all shareholders are individuals

Exemption amounts (YA 2020 onwards):
- 75% exemption on first SGD 100,000 of chargeable income
- 50% exemption on next SGD 100,000 of chargeable income
- Maximum exemption: SGD 125,000 per YA

NOT eligible for SUTE:
- Investment holding companies
- Property development companies
- Companies incorporated outside Singapore

Partial Tax Exemption (PTE)
For all other companies not qualifying for SUTE.
Exemption amounts:
- 75% exemption on first SGD 10,000 of chargeable income
- 50% exemption on next SGD 190,000 of chargeable income
- Maximum exemption: SGD 102,500 per YA

---

CORPORATE INCOME TAX (CIT) REBATE — YA 2026

For Year of Assessment 2026:
- CIT rebate: 40% of tax payable, capped at SGD 30,000
- CIT rebate cash grant: SGD 1,500 for companies with at least one local employee (Singapore citizen or PR) who has CPF contributions for that employment
- The cash grant and rebate combined cannot exceed SGD 30,000 per YA
- The SGD 1,500 cash grant is paid regardless of whether the company has tax payable

How to determine YA 2026:
- Companies with financial year ending between 1 January 2025 and 31 December 2025 have YA 2026.

---

YEAR OF ASSESSMENT (YA)

The Year of Assessment is the year in which income tax is assessed.
The basis period for YA 2026 is generally the financial year ending in 2025.
Income is assessed based on the accounting profits for the basis period.

---

IRAS FORM TYPES

Form C-S Lite:
- For companies with annual revenue of SGD 200,000 or below
- Simplest form; no financial statements required to be submitted
- Available for YA 2020 onwards

Form C-S:
- For companies with annual revenue of SGD 5 million or below
- Company must be incorporated in Singapore
- No capital allowances on renovation/refurbishment claims, no carry-back of losses
- Shorter form; no financial statements required to be submitted

Form C:
- For all other companies not qualifying for C-S Lite or C-S
- Revenue exceeds SGD 5 million, or has complex tax matters
- Full form; audited/unaudited financial statements and tax computation required

---

FILING DEADLINES

Estimated Chargeable Income (ECI):
- Must be filed within 3 months after the end of the company's financial year
- ECI is an estimate of the company's chargeable income for that basis period
- Companies with annual revenue ≤ SGD 5 million and chargeable income of nil may be exempt from filing ECI

Corporate Tax Return (Form C-S / C-S Lite / Form C):
- Filing deadline: 30 November of the Year of Assessment
- e-Filing via myTax Portal using CorpPass

Payment:
- IRAS issues the Notice of Assessment after reviewing the return
- Tax payable within 1 month from the date of assessment
- Instalment payment plans (GIRO) available — up to 10 monthly instalments

---

DOUBLE TAX AGREEMENTS (DTAs)

Singapore has an extensive network of over 80 DTAs with other countries.
DTAs reduce or eliminate withholding tax on dividends, interest, and royalties paid to foreign entities.
Tax credits available for foreign taxes paid on income also taxed in Singapore.

---

WITHHOLDING TAX

Singapore companies paying certain types of income to non-residents must withhold tax:
- Interest: 15%
- Royalties: 10%
- Technical assistance fees: 17% (or prevailing CIT rate)
- Director's fees for non-resident directors: 24% (or prevailing rate)
- Payments to non-resident professionals: 15% on gross or 22% on net
Reduced rates may apply under DTAs.

---

GOODS AND SERVICES TAX (GST)

GST is a separate tax from corporate income tax.
Current GST rate: 9% (effective 1 January 2024).
Compulsory GST registration: when taxable turnover exceeds SGD 1 million in the past 12 months or is expected to exceed SGD 1 million in the next 12 months.
Voluntary GST registration: available for businesses below the threshold.
GST-registered businesses must file GST returns quarterly (most common) or monthly.

---

STARTUP TAX INCENTIVE — ANGEL INVESTOR

Angel Investors Tax Deduction (AITD) scheme: allows approved angel investors to claim 50% tax deduction on qualifying investments made in approved start-up companies.
