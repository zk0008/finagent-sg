Singapore Audit Exemption — Small Company and Exempt Private Company Rules

SOURCE: Companies Act (Cap. 50), ACRA, Singapore

---

AUDIT EXEMPTION OVERVIEW

Singapore introduced audit exemption for small companies under the Companies (Amendment) Act 2014, effective from financial years beginning on or after 1 July 2015.

The purpose is to reduce the compliance burden on small businesses while maintaining appropriate financial reporting standards.

---

SMALL COMPANY CRITERIA

A private company qualifies as a "small company" and is exempt from statutory audit if it meets at least 2 of the following 3 quantitative criteria for the immediate past 2 consecutive financial years:

Criterion 1: Annual revenue not more than SGD 10 million
Criterion 2: Total assets (gross) not more than SGD 10 million
Criterion 3: Number of employees not more than 50 (full-time employees at end of financial year)

Key points:
- Must satisfy at least 2 out of 3 criteria
- Must be satisfied for 2 consecutive financial years
- Exception: a company newly incorporated that meets 2 of 3 criteria in its first financial year is exempt for that first year

---

SMALL COMPANY — FIRST YEAR OF INCORPORATION

For a newly incorporated company in its first financial year:
- Deemed to satisfy the small company criteria if it meets at least 2 of the 3 criteria for that year
- Does not need 2 consecutive years for the first year

---

LOSING SMALL COMPANY STATUS

A company that previously qualified as a small company will lose its exemption if it fails to meet at least 2 of 3 criteria for 2 consecutive financial years.

---

SMALL GROUP CRITERIA (FOR COMPANIES IN A GROUP)

A company that is part of a group must also qualify as a "small group" in addition to being a small company.

A group is a "small group" if it meets at least 2 of the following 3 criteria for the group as a whole for the immediate past 2 consecutive financial years:

1. Consolidated annual revenue not more than SGD 10 million
2. Consolidated total assets not more than SGD 10 million
3. Total number of employees across the group not more than 50

If a company is a small company but its group does not qualify as a small group, the company still requires a statutory audit.

---

COMPANIES THAT CANNOT CLAIM AUDIT EXEMPTION

The following types of companies are NOT eligible for the small company audit exemption regardless of size:

- Public companies (listed or unlisted)
- Subsidiaries of public companies
- Companies regulated by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS):
  - Licensed banks
  - Insurance companies
  - Capital markets services licence holders
  - Finance companies
  - Moneylenders
- Companies whose securities are listed on a stock exchange

---

SOLVENCY STATEMENT

When a company is exempt from audit, the directors must lodge a solvency statement with ACRA as part of the annual return.

The solvency statement confirms that:
- The company will be able to pay its debts as and when they become due during the 12 months immediately following the date of the annual return

This is a directors' responsibility and replaces the assurance previously provided by the statutory audit.

---

EXEMPT PRIVATE COMPANY (EPC) — ADDITIONAL EXEMPTIONS

An Exempt Private Company (EPC) is a private company where:
- No shares are held by a corporation
- Has 20 or fewer shareholders
- All shareholders are individuals (not corporate entities)

EPCs that are also small companies have additional filing exemptions:
- Not required to file financial statements with ACRA if annual revenue is below SGD 5 million
- Must still maintain financial records and prepare financial statements (they are just not required to be publicly filed)

EPCs with revenue between SGD 5 million and SGD 10 million:
- Must file financial statements with annual return
- Exempt from audit if they qualify as a small company

---

FINANCIAL STATEMENTS STILL REQUIRED

Even if a company is exempt from statutory audit, it must still:
1. Prepare financial statements in accordance with SFRS (or SFRS for Small Entities if eligible)
2. Present financial statements to shareholders at the AGM (or by written resolution in lieu of AGM)
3. Keep proper accounting records for at least 5 years

The audit exemption only removes the requirement for an independent external audit — it does not remove the obligation to prepare and present financial statements.

---

SFRS FOR SMALL ENTITIES

Small companies (as defined above) may choose to adopt SFRS for Small Entities (SFRS for SE) instead of full SFRS.
SFRS for SE is a simplified set of standards with reduced disclosure requirements.
It is based on the IASB's IFRS for SMEs.
Not all SFRS standards apply — many are simplified or eliminated.

Eligibility for SFRS for SE:
- Must be a small company (meets 2 of 3 criteria)
- Cannot be publicly accountable (not listed, not a financial institution)
- Shareholders with > 5% voting rights must not object

---

DETERMINING REVENUE AND TOTAL ASSETS

Revenue: turnover from ordinary activities of the company (excluding GST).
For trading companies: gross sales
For service companies: fee income
For investment holding companies: dividend income and interest income

Total Assets: the gross total assets as shown on the balance sheet at financial year end.
Does not net off liabilities — it is the total of all assets.

Number of Employees: total number of full-time employees (and part-time employees converted to full-time equivalent).
Employees at end of financial year.
