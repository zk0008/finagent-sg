CPF Contribution Rates and Rules for Singapore Employers

SOURCE: Central Provident Fund Board (CPFB), CPF Act, Ministry of Manpower (MOM)

---

WHO MUST CONTRIBUTE TO CPF

CPF contributions are mandatory for:
- Singapore citizens employed in Singapore
- Singapore permanent residents (PRs) employed in Singapore

CPF is NOT required for:
- Foreign employees (Employment Pass, S Pass, Work Permit holders)
- Self-employed persons (SEPs contribute to Medisave separately)
- Employees earning less than SGD 50 per month

---

CPF CONTRIBUTION RATES — EFFECTIVE 1 JANUARY 2026

For Singapore Citizens aged 55 and below:
- Employer contribution: 17% of ordinary wages (OW)
- Employee contribution: 20% of ordinary wages (OW)
- Total CPF contribution: 37% of ordinary wages

For Singapore Citizens aged above 55 to 60:
- Employer contribution: 15% of OW
- Employee contribution: 16% of OW
- Total: 31% of OW

For Singapore Citizens aged above 60 to 65:
- Employer contribution: 11.5% of OW
- Employee contribution: 10.5% of OW
- Total: 22% of OW

For Singapore Citizens aged above 65 to 70:
- Employer contribution: 9% of OW
- Employee contribution: 7.5% of OW
- Total: 16.5% of OW

For Singapore Citizens aged above 70:
- Employer contribution: 7.5% of OW
- Employee contribution: 5% of OW
- Total: 12.5% of OW

Singapore Permanent Residents (SPR) — 1st year of PR status:
- Employer contribution: 4% of OW
- Employee contribution: 5% of OW
(Graduated rates apply for first 2 years; full rates from 3rd year)

---

ORDINARY WAGES (OW) AND ADDITIONAL WAGES (AW)

Ordinary Wages (OW):
- Wages due or granted in respect of employment for the month
- Includes basic salary, allowances that are fixed and regularly paid each month
- Does NOT include overtime pay, bonuses, AWS (13th month), or other non-recurring payments

Ordinary Wage Ceiling:
- The monthly OW ceiling for CPF contributions is SGD 8,000 from 1 January 2026
- Previously SGD 6,800 (2024), increasing progressively to SGD 8,000 by 2026
- CPF is computed on OW up to the ceiling only
- OW exceeding SGD 8,000 per month is NOT subject to CPF

Additional Wages (AW):
- Wages paid at irregular intervals — bonuses, AWS, commissions, overtime pay
- Subject to CPF up to the AW ceiling

Annual Wage Supplement (AWS) / 13th Month Bonus:
- Treated as additional wages
- CPF payable on AWS up to the AW ceiling for the year

Additional Wage (AW) Ceiling:
- AW ceiling = SGD 102,000 - total OW subject to CPF for the year
- This ensures total CPF-liable wages are capped at SGD 102,000 per year

---

CPF ALLOCATION RATES (for members aged 55 and below)

Total CPF contributions are allocated across three accounts:
- Ordinary Account (OA): 23% of wages (used for housing, education, investment)
- Special Account (SA): 6% of wages (used for retirement)
- Medisave Account (MA): 8% of wages (used for medical expenses)

Note: Exact allocation varies with age; older workers have higher Medisave and Special Account allocations.

---

SKILL DEVELOPMENT LEVY (SDL)

All employers must pay SDL for all employees (including foreign employees).
Rate: 0.25% of gross monthly remuneration for each employee
Minimum: SGD 2 per employee per month
Maximum: SGD 11.25 per employee per month (for salaries above SGD 4,500)
SDL is used to fund the SkillsFuture Singapore (SSG) programmes.

SDL is payable together with CPF contributions via CPF e-Submit.

---

EMPLOYER CPF PAYMENT

CPF contributions must be paid by the 14th of the following month.
Payment via CPF e-Submit@web or e-Submit@AXS.
Late payment attracts late payment interest at 1.5% per month (18% per annum), minimum SGD 5.

---

CPF SUBMISSION

Employers must submit CPF contributions monthly via CPF e-Submit.
For salary months in which CPF is due, submission and payment must be made together.
The CPF e-Submit file format is a CSV with specific column requirements:
- Employee NRIC/FIN, name, amount of OW, amount of AW, employer CPF, employee CPF
- SDL amount is automatically calculated by CPF Board

---

PAYSLIP REQUIREMENTS (MOM REGULATIONS)

Under the Employment Act, employers must issue itemised payslips to all employees covered by the Act.
Payslip must include:
- Employer name and employee name
- Employee's designation
- Date of payment and payment period covered
- Basic salary and allowances paid
- Any deductions made
- Overtime pay
- Net salary paid
- CPF contribution (employer and employee amounts)

Payslips must be issued together with salary payment or within 3 working days of salary payment.
Employers must keep payslip records for at least 2 years.

---

NS MAKE-UP PAY

Employers must pay Make-Up Pay to employees on National Service (NS) to top up their NS allowance to their regular civilian pay.
Employers can claim reimbursement from MINDEF for the Make-Up Pay.

---

MATERNITY / PATERNITY LEAVE

Government-paid maternity leave: 16 weeks for Singapore citizen children; 8 weeks for non-citizen children.
Government-paid paternity leave: 4 weeks for Singapore citizen children.
Shared parental leave: available in addition to paternity leave.
Employers pay during leave period and claim reimbursement from Government for Government-paid portions.
