SFRS Accounting Policies and Notes to Financial Statements

SOURCE: SFRS 1-1 Presentation of Financial Statements, Accounting Standards Council Singapore

---

ACCOUNTING POLICIES

Accounting policies are the specific principles, bases, conventions, rules and practices applied by an entity in preparing and presenting financial statements.

Selection of accounting policies:
When a SFRS applies to a transaction, the accounting policy must be selected by applying that SFRS.
When no SFRS applies specifically, management uses judgement to develop a policy that results in relevant and reliable information.

Consistency:
An entity must apply its accounting policies consistently across periods.
Changes are permitted only if required by a new SFRS or if the change results in more reliable and relevant financial information.

---

NOTES TO FINANCIAL STATEMENTS — REQUIRED DISCLOSURES

1. BASIS OF PREPARATION
State that the financial statements have been prepared in accordance with SFRS (or SFRS for SE).
State the measurement basis (historical cost, or other if used).
State the functional and presentation currency.

2. SIGNIFICANT ACCOUNTING POLICIES
Disclose all accounting policies relevant to understanding the financial statements, including:
- Revenue recognition policy
- Basis for consolidation (for group accounts)
- Basis for translation of foreign currencies
- PPE — measurement basis and depreciation methods and rates
- Intangible assets — measurement basis and amortisation methods
- Financial instruments — classification and measurement
- Inventories — cost formula and measurement basis
- Leases — lessee accounting policy
- Income taxes — current and deferred tax policy
- Provisions and contingencies

3. SOURCES OF ESTIMATION UNCERTAINTY
Disclose key assumptions about the future and major sources of estimation uncertainty that have a significant risk of resulting in material adjustments within the next financial year.
Examples: useful life of PPE, recoverability of deferred tax assets, outcome of pending litigation.

4. REVENUE
Disclose revenue broken down by type or major category.
Show disaggregation of revenue that depicts how the nature, amount, timing, and uncertainty of revenue and cash flows are affected by economic factors.
Disclose significant judgements made in applying SFRS 15.

5. PROPERTY, PLANT AND EQUIPMENT
For each class of PPE, disclose:
- Gross carrying amount and accumulated depreciation at beginning and end of period
- Additions, disposals, depreciation charge, and impairment losses
- Useful lives or depreciation rates
- Depreciation method used
- Net carrying amount at end of period

6. TRADE AND OTHER RECEIVABLES
Breakdown of receivables: trade receivables, other receivables, deposits, prepayments.
Allowance for impairment — movement during the year.
Credit risk concentration.

7. INVENTORIES
Cost formula used (FIFO or weighted average).
Carrying amount by classification (raw materials, WIP, finished goods).
Amount of inventories recognised as expense (cost of goods sold).
Amount of any write-down to NRV.

8. TRADE AND OTHER PAYABLES
Breakdown: trade payables, accruals, other payables.
Currency analysis if significant foreign currency exposure.

9. LOANS AND BORROWINGS
For each loan: amount, interest rate, maturity date, security provided.
Repayment schedule — amounts due within 1 year and after 1 year.
Covenants.

10. INCOME TAX
Current tax — current year charge and prior year adjustments.
Deferred tax — movements in deferred tax assets and liabilities.
Reconciliation of effective tax rate to statutory rate.
Deferred tax balances by category.

11. SHARE CAPITAL
Number and class of shares authorised (if applicable) and issued.
Movement in share capital.
Rights, preferences, restrictions attached to each class.

12. RETAINED EARNINGS / DIVIDENDS
Movement in retained earnings.
Dividends declared or paid during the year.
Dividends declared after reporting date but before financial statements authorised for issue (non-adjusting event; disclose).

13. CONTINGENCIES AND COMMITMENTS
Contingent liabilities — description, estimate of financial effect, uncertainties.
Capital commitments — contracted but not provided for in financial statements.
Operating lease commitments (for pre-SFRS 16 short-term/low value lease exemptions).

14. SUBSEQUENT EVENTS
Events after the reporting date (adjusting and non-adjusting) up to the date financial statements are authorised for issue.
Date financial statements were authorised for issue and who gave the authorisation.

---

GOING CONCERN DISCLOSURE

If there are material uncertainties relating to going concern, these must be disclosed prominently.
Even if the directors conclude going concern is appropriate, material uncertainties must be described.
If the company is no longer a going concern, the basis of preparation changes and this must be clearly disclosed.

---

DIRECTORS' STATEMENT

Companies must include a Directors' Statement which states:
1. In the directors' opinion, the financial statements give a true and fair view of the financial position and performance
2. At the date of the statement, there are reasonable grounds to believe the company can pay its debts as and when they become due
3. Disclosure of any arrangements for directors to acquire shares or debentures

---

AUTHORISATION DATE

Financial statements must disclose the date they were authorised for issue.
Events occurring after this date are not required to be reflected or disclosed.
