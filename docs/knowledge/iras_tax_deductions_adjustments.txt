IRAS Tax Deductions and Common Tax Adjustments for Singapore Companies

SOURCE: Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore (IRAS), Income Tax Act (ITA), Singapore

---

GENERAL DEDUCTIBILITY PRINCIPLE (SECTION 14, ITA)

An expense is tax-deductible if it is:
- Revenue (not capital) in nature
- Wholly and exclusively incurred in the production of income
- Not specifically prohibited by the ITA

If an expense fails any of these tests, it must be added back to arrive at chargeable income.

---

COMMON ADD-BACKS (NON-DEDUCTIBLE EXPENSES)

These items are added back to accounting profit when computing chargeable income:

1. DEPRECIATION
   - Accounting depreciation is not tax-deductible
   - Replaced by capital allowances under Section 19/19A of the ITA
   - Full add-back required; then capital allowances claimed separately

2. PRIVATE MOTOR VEHICLE EXPENSES (S-PLATE / Q-PLATE CARS)
   - S-plate and Q-plate cars are classified as "S-plated" private vehicles
   - 100% of all expenses relating to S-plate cars are disallowed:
     - Depreciation
     - Petrol and road tax
     - Repairs and maintenance
     - Car park charges
     - Motor insurance
   - Exception: hire car businesses (taxis, rental companies) where S-plate cars are core business assets
   - Commercial vehicles (vans, lorries) are fully deductible

3. ENTERTAINMENT EXPENSES
   - Entertainment expenses are deductible ONLY to the extent they are wholly and exclusively incurred in the production of income
   - In practice, IRAS allows 100% deduction for bona fide business entertainment
   - However, entertainment for personal benefit of directors/employees is disallowed
   - Common partial disallowance: meals where no business purpose demonstrated
   - Food and beverage for employees at the workplace (e.g. office pantry) is generally deductible

4. FINES AND PENALTIES
   - Fines and penalties imposed by law are not deductible
   - Examples: GST penalties, traffic fines, late payment penalties to IRAS
   - Interest on overdue taxes is also not deductible

5. DONATIONS
   - Cash donations to non-approved institutions are not deductible
   - Donations to approved Institutions of Public Character (IPCs) attract 250% tax deduction
   - In-kind donations also eligible for tax deduction if made to IPCs

6. CAPITAL EXPENDITURE
   - Capital expenditure is not revenue in nature — not deductible
   - Instead, capital allowances may be claimed on qualifying plant and machinery
   - Examples of capital expenditure: purchase of machinery, renovation costs, purchase of IP

7. PROVISIONS
   - General provisions are not deductible (e.g. general bad debt provisions)
   - Specific provisions are deductible when the debt is irrecoverable and written off

8. PERSONAL EXPENSES OF DIRECTORS/SHAREHOLDERS
   - Expenses of a personal nature incurred by directors or shareholders charged to the company
   - Examples: personal travel, personal insurance, school fees

9. NON-BUSINESS EXPENSES
   - Expenses not incurred in producing income are disallowed
   - Examples: expenses for investment activities if investment income is exempt

---

COMMON DEDUCTIONS (TAX-ALLOWABLE EXPENSES)

1. STAFF COSTS
   - Salaries, wages, and bonuses paid to employees — fully deductible
   - Employer CPF contributions — fully deductible
   - Staff training costs — fully deductible
   - Staff welfare costs (within reasonable limits)

2. RENTAL OF BUSINESS PREMISES
   - Office/factory/warehouse rental is fully deductible

3. UTILITIES AND COMMUNICATION
   - Electricity, water, telephone, internet — fully deductible if for business use

4. PROFESSIONAL FEES
   - Accounting and audit fees — deductible
   - Legal fees for revenue matters — deductible
   - Legal fees for capital matters (e.g. acquisition of property) — not deductible

5. REPAIRS AND MAINTENANCE
   - Revenue repairs to maintain existing assets — deductible
   - Capital improvements that enhance value — not deductible (capital allowances may apply)

6. INTEREST EXPENSE
   - Interest on loans used to produce income — deductible (Section 14(1)(a))
   - Interest on loans for investment in subsidiary — deductible if dividend income is taxable
   - Interest on loans for private purpose — not deductible

7. INSURANCE
   - Business insurance premiums — deductible
   - Key-man insurance (where company is beneficiary) — deductible
   - Private motor vehicle insurance — not deductible (S-plate car rule)

8. BAD DEBTS
   - Trade debts written off — deductible if:
     (a) The debt was previously included in income
     (b) The debt has been genuinely written off as irrecoverable
   - Specific provisions for identified bad debts — deductible when reasonable grounds exist

CAPITAL ALLOWANCES (SECTIONS 19 AND 19A, ITA)

Capital allowances replace accounting depreciation as the tax deduction for plant and machinery.

Qualifying plant and machinery: assets used in the production of income, not land or buildings.

Types of capital allowances:

1-Year Write-Off (Section 19A(1A)):
- For qualifying low-value assets costing SGD 5,000 or less per item
- Maximum total claim: SGD 30,000 per year of assessment

3-Year Write-Off (Section 19A(1)):
- 33.3% per year for 3 years
- Most common for IT equipment, office equipment, machinery

Accelerated Capital Allowances (Section 19B):
- For energy-efficient and environment-friendly equipment
- 1-year or 3-year write-off available

Writing Down Allowances (Section 19):
- Over the prescribed working life of the asset (typically 10 years)
- Less commonly used; requires submission to IRAS

Renovation and Refurbishment (R&R) Allowances:
- Qualifying R&R expenses deducted over 3 years (one-third each year)
- Cap: SGD 300,000 per 3-year period

---

STARTUP COSTS

Pre-commencement expenses incurred within 12 months before the start of business:
- Revenue expenses are deductible in the first year of assessment
- Example: market research costs, advertising before opening, professional fees for setup

---

CARRY-FORWARD OF LOSSES

Unabsorbed losses may be carried forward indefinitely to offset future taxable income.
Condition: there must be no substantial change in shareholders (50% shareholding continuity test).

Carry-back of losses:
- Permitted for up to 1 year of assessment (SGD 100,000 cap)
- Allows losses to be offset against prior year's assessable income for immediate refund

---

SECTION 14C/14D — R&D EXPENDITURE

Qualifying R&D expenditure may qualify for enhanced deduction:
- 150% deduction for qualifying R&D expenditure incurred in Singapore
- Must relate to the trade or business of the company
- Applies to staff costs and consumables used in R&D

---

PRODUCTIVITY AND INNOVATION CREDIT (PIC) — HISTORICAL

PIC expired after YA 2018. Companies that claimed PIC in prior years should ensure no ongoing clawback.
