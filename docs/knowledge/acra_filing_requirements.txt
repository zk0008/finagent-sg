ACRA Filing Requirements for Singapore Private Limited Companies

SOURCE: Accounting and Corporate Regulatory Authority (ACRA), Companies Act (Cap. 50), Singapore

---

ANNUAL RETURN FILING

All Singapore-incorporated companies must file an annual return with ACRA via BizFile+.

Deadline:
- Private companies: within 7 months after the financial year end (FYE)
- Public companies: within 5 months after the FYE

The annual return must include:
- Confirmation of the company's particulars (registered address, directors, shareholders, share capital)
- Financial statements (unless exempted)
- Solvency statement (for companies exempt from audit)

Filing fee: SGD 60 per annual return (as at 2026).

---

AUDIT EXEMPTION — SMALL COMPANY CRITERIA

A private company qualifies for audit exemption as a "small company" if it meets at least 2 of the following 3 criteria for the immediate past 2 consecutive financial years:

1. Annual revenue not more than SGD 10 million
2. Total assets not more than SGD 10 million
3. Number of employees not more than 50

A company that qualifies as small in its first financial year is also exempt for that year.

A small company that is part of a group must also satisfy the small group criteria:
- The group as a whole meets at least 2 of 3 criteria: revenue ≤ $10M, assets ≤ $10M, employees ≤ 50

Companies that CANNOT be exempt from audit regardless of size:
- Public companies
- Subsidiary of a public company
- Companies regulated by MAS (banks, insurers, capital market intermediaries)
- Companies listed on a securities exchange

---

EXEMPT PRIVATE COMPANY (EPC)

An exempt private company (EPC) is a private company with:
- No more than 20 shareholders
- No corporate shareholders

EPCs have additional filing exemptions:
- Exempt from filing financial statements with annual return if revenue is below SGD 5 million
- Directors must still lodge a solvency statement confirming the company can pay its debts

---

FILING FINANCIAL STATEMENTS WITH ACRA

Companies required to file financial statements must submit them in XBRL format via BizFile+.

XBRL filing requirements:
- Full XBRL: companies with revenue > SGD 500,000 that are not small companies
- Simplified XBRL: insolvent EPCs with revenue ≤ SGD 500,000
- Financial statement highlights: some EPC categories

Dormant companies: exempt from preparing and filing financial statements if they have been dormant since incorporation or since the end of the previous financial year.

---

REGISTER OF CONTROLLERS

All Singapore companies must maintain a Register of Registrable Controllers (RORC) and lodge it with ACRA.
Controllers: individuals or legal entities with significant interest (≥25% of shares or votes) or significant control.
Must be lodged within 2 business days of any changes.

---

REGISTER OF NOMINEE DIRECTORS

Companies with nominee directors must maintain a register identifying the nominators.
This was introduced under the Companies (Amendment) Act to improve corporate transparency.

---

CHANGES TO COMPANY PARTICULARS

Changes must be filed with ACRA within the required timeframes:
- Change of company name: file before using the new name
- Change of registered office address: within 14 days
- Change of director/secretary particulars: within 14 days
- Allotment of new shares: within 14 days
- Transfer of shares: within 14 days of registration

---

STRIKING OFF

ACRA may strike off a company that is:
- Not carrying on business
- Not in operation

A company may apply for voluntary striking off if:
- Has not commenced business or has ceased business
- Has no assets or liabilities
- Not involved in any legal proceedings
- Not subject to any ongoing IRAS investigations

---

BUSINESS PROFILE

A Business Profile (BizFile+ extract) is the official record of a company's registration details.
Used for opening bank accounts, applying for licences, and signing contracts.
Can be purchased from BizFile+ at any time for a small fee.

---

COMPANY SECRETARY

Every Singapore company must appoint a company secretary within 6 months of incorporation.
The company secretary must be a resident of Singapore.
The sole director of a company cannot also be the company secretary.
The company secretary maintains statutory registers, ensures timely filing, and advises on compliance.

---

DIRECTORS

A Singapore company must have at least one director who is ordinarily resident in Singapore.
Directors must be at least 18 years old and not bankrupt.
Sole director must not also be the company secretary.
