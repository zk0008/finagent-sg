SFRS Financial Statements Requirements for Singapore Private Limited Companies

SOURCE: Singapore Financial Reporting Standards (SFRS), ACRA

---

REQUIRED FINANCIAL STATEMENTS

A Singapore private limited company must prepare a complete set of financial statements comprising:

1. Statement of Financial Position (Balance Sheet)
   - Shows assets, liabilities, and equity at the reporting date
   - Assets and liabilities classified as current or non-current

2. Statement of Profit or Loss and Other Comprehensive Income (Income Statement / P&L)
   - Shows revenue, expenses, and profit or loss for the period
   - Other comprehensive income items presented separately

3. Statement of Cash Flows
   - Shows cash inflows and outflows from operating, investing, and financing activities
   - Direct or indirect method permitted; indirect method more commonly used in Singapore

4. Statement of Changes in Equity
   - Shows movements in share capital, retained earnings, and reserves during the period

5. Notes to the Financial Statements
   - Accounting policies
   - Supporting detail for each line item
   - Contingencies and commitments
   - Related party disclosures
   - Subsequent events

---

CURRENT VS NON-CURRENT CLASSIFICATION (SFRS 1-1)

Current Assets
An asset is classified as current when it meets any of the following criteria:
- Expected to be realised, sold, or consumed within the normal operating cycle
- Held primarily for the purpose of trading
- Expected to be realised within 12 months after the reporting date
- Cash or cash equivalent (unless restricted for more than 12 months)

Examples of current assets: cash and bank balances, trade receivables, inventories, prepayments, short-term deposits

Non-Current Assets
All other assets that do not meet the current asset criteria are classified as non-current.
- Held for longer-term use in the business
- Not expected to be realised within 12 months

Examples of non-current assets: property, plant and equipment (PPE), intangible assets, investment properties, long-term deposits, deferred tax assets

Current Liabilities
A liability is classified as current when:
- Expected to be settled within the normal operating cycle
- Held primarily for the purpose of trading
- Due to be settled within 12 months after the reporting date
- The company does not have an unconditional right to defer settlement beyond 12 months

Examples: trade payables, accruals, short-term borrowings, current portion of long-term loans, tax payable

Non-Current Liabilities
All liabilities not classified as current.
Examples: long-term bank loans, deferred tax liabilities, finance lease liabilities

---

GOING CONCERN

Directors must assess the company's ability to continue as a going concern at each reporting date.
If there is material uncertainty, this must be disclosed in the notes.
If the company is not a going concern, financial statements must be prepared on a different basis (e.g. liquidation basis) and this must be disclosed.

---

PRESENTATION CURRENCY

Singapore companies typically present financial statements in Singapore Dollars (SGD).
A company may present in a different currency if that is its functional currency, but must disclose this.

---

COMPARATIVE INFORMATION

Financial statements must include comparative information for the preceding period for all amounts reported.
This applies to the balance sheet, P&L, cash flow statement, and statement of changes in equity.

---

REPORTING DATE

Most Singapore companies have a financial year end (FYE) of 31 December, 30 June, or 31 March, but any date is permitted.
The FYE is registered with ACRA and determines the filing deadline for the annual return.

---

MATERIALITY

Information is material if its omission or misstatement could influence the economic decisions of users.
Immaterial items need not be separately disclosed but must not be used to obscure material information.
