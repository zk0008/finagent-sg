Related Party Disclosures — SFRS 24

SOURCE: Singapore Financial Reporting Standard 1-24 (SFRS 1-24), Accounting Standards Council Singapore

---

OBJECTIVE

SFRS 1-24 requires disclosure of related party relationships, transactions, and outstanding balances so that users of financial statements can understand how the reporting entity may be affected by related parties.

---

DEFINITION OF RELATED PARTY

A party is related to an entity if:
- The party is a person or a close member of that person's family if that person:
  (a) has control or joint control over the reporting entity
  (b) has significant influence over the reporting entity
  (c) is a member of the key management personnel of the reporting entity or its parent

- The party is an entity where any of the following conditions apply:
  (a) The entity and the reporting entity are members of the same group
  (b) One entity is an associate or joint venture of the other
  (c) Both entities are joint ventures of the same third party
  (d) One entity is a joint venture of a third entity and the other is an associate of that same third entity
  (e) The entity is a post-employment benefit plan for the employees of either the reporting entity or a related entity
  (f) The entity is controlled or jointly controlled by a person identified above
  (g) A person identified in (a) above has significant influence over the entity or is a member of its key management personnel
  (h) The entity, or any member of a group of which it is a part, provides key management personnel services to the reporting entity or its parent

Common related parties for Singapore private limited companies:
- Shareholders (especially controlling shareholders)
- Directors and their family members
- Subsidiaries, associates, and joint ventures
- Entities controlled by directors or shareholders
- Key management personnel

---

REQUIRED DISCLOSURES

Relationships
Even if there were no transactions, disclose the name of the entity's parent and ultimate controlling party.
Disclose the nature of the relationship when the entity is controlled by related parties.

Transactions
If transactions occurred between the entity and its related parties, disclose:
1. The nature of the related party relationship
2. Information about the transactions — sufficient to understand the potential effect on the financial statements
3. The amount of the transactions
4. Outstanding balances at reporting date — terms and conditions, whether secured, settlement terms
5. Provisions for doubtful debts related to outstanding balances
6. Expense recognised in the period for bad or doubtful debts due from related parties

Types of related party transactions common in Singapore SMEs:
- Director's fees and remuneration
- Director's loans to/from the company
- Loans to/from shareholders
- Management fees charged by related entities
- Rental of property to/from related parties
- Sales of goods or services to related entities
- Purchase of goods or services from related entities
- Guarantees provided by directors or shareholders

---

KEY MANAGEMENT PERSONNEL (KMP)

Key management personnel are those persons having authority and responsibility for planning, directing, and controlling activities of the entity — typically directors and senior management.

Disclosures required for KMP compensation:
- Short-term employee benefits (salaries, wages, bonuses, CPF contributions)
- Post-employment benefits
- Other long-term benefits
- Termination benefits
- Share-based payments

Total KMP compensation must be disclosed, broken into each category above.

---

GOVERNMENT-RELATED ENTITIES

Entities that are controlled, jointly controlled, or significantly influenced by a government are exempt from some disclosure requirements when transacting with other government-related entities (the government-related entity exemption). This typically applies to GLCs and statutory boards, not private limited companies.

---

ARMS-LENGTH TRANSACTIONS

The standard does not require disclosure of whether transactions were made on an arm's-length basis unless the entity can substantiate this claim. Simply stating "transactions were at arm's length" without substantiation is not appropriate.

---

PRACTICAL APPLICATION FOR SINGAPORE SMEs

For small Singapore private limited companies, common related party disclosures include:
- Director's salaries, CPF, and bonuses paid during the year
- Director's current account balances (amounts owed to/from directors)
- Loans from shareholders (interest-free or otherwise)
- Transactions with companies owned by the same directors/shareholders
- Guarantees from directors for company bank facilities

Note: Related party transactions are a key area of focus for ACRA and auditors. Ensure all related party transactions are identified and properly disclosed even if they appear minor.
